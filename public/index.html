By Dr. Sarah Chen · March 15, 2026 · 8 min read

A team of marine biologists from the Monterey Bay Aquarium Research Institute announced yesterday the discovery of a previously unknown species of bioluminescent jellyfish living at depths exceeding 4,000 meters in the Pacific Ocean. The organism, tentatively named Aurelia profundis, produces a unique protein compound that early laboratory testing suggests could have significant pharmaceutical applications.

"We've been studying deep-sea organisms for decades, but nothing has quite compared to this," said lead researcher Dr. Maria Santos during a press conference at the institute. "The protein structures we're observing are unlike anything currently documented in marine biology literature."
